Zarina Baloch: The Jee Jee (Mother) of Sindhi Peopleh

Zarina Baloch (Sindhi: زرينه بلوچ‎) (born on the day of December 29, 1934 – died on October 25, 2005) was a popular Pakistani folk music singer, vocalist and composer. She was also a well-known actress, Radio and TV artist, teacher, writer, political and social worker.

Early life and family of Zarina Baloch

She was born on the day of December 29, 1934 in the area of Allahdad Chand Village, Hyderabad, Sindh province. Her mother, Gulroz, breathed last in the year of 1940 when Zarina was only 6 years old. She acquired education with Mohammad Juman, who was also a popular Sindhi singer. At early age 15 years, her family decided her marriage with a remote relative. She had 2 kids: Akhter Baloch also famous to be known as Zeena (born in the year of 1952), and Asalm Parvez (born in the year of 1957). Although, Baloch and her husband disagreed on the topic of her higher education and the pair were separated on the year of 1958.
Baloch then joined Radio Hyderabad in the year of 1960 and gained her 1st Music Award in the year of 1961. Then Zarina married a renowned Sindhi politician Rasool Bux Palijo, took place in the city of Hyderabad on the day of September 22, 1964 and they had a son, Ayaz Latif Palijo. In the 1967, she became a teacher at the Model School Sindh University. She retired in the year of 1997 and lost her life in 2005 due to Brain Cancer in Liaquat National Hospital.

Imprisonment and political activism

In the year of 1979, Zarina was arrested and imprisoned in the city of Sukkur and Karachi jails for leading the protests or strikes against President General Zia ul Haq’s Martial Law. The patriotic songs and anthems sung by Zarina Baloch “Maan Chuke Balochani”, “Man Yagehaan” and “Jeaey Sindh” became the musical signs of Baloch and Sindhi nationalism. Due to her great efforts against the ruling classes, gender discrimination, feudalism and martial laws, she acquired the title of Jee Jee (mother) of the Sindhi people. She was believed to be one of the leading founders of Sindhiani Tahreek, Sindhi Adabi Sangat, Women’s Action Forum, and Sindhi Haree Committee. She was very fluent in the languages of Sindhi, Urdu, Seraiki, Balochi, Persian, Arabic and Gujrati.

Awards

Her recognitions involve: the Waheed Murad Award, the Shah Latif Award, the Lal Shahbaz Award, the Sachal Award, the Shah Sachal Sami Award, the SGA Award, the WSC Award, the Ram Panjwani Award, the PTV Award, the Faiz Ahmed Faiz Award, the Sindhi Sadaeen Gad Award and the Pride of Performance Award.

Art and literary contributions

She wrote various songs and poetry which became famous among the nationalists in the provinces of Sindh and Baluchistan. She was the author of several stories and poems, and her Book “Tunhinjee Gola Tunhinjoon Galhion” was released in the year of 1992.
