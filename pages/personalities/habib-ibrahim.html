Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola: The 8th Governor of Sindh, Pakistan

Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola (March 12, 1912 – January 2, 1991) was the 1st High Commissioner to the UK for the state Pakistan. He was also the eighth and fifth Governor of Sindh and Punjab respectively.

Early life and political career of Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola

Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola completed his early education at the institute of St. Xavier’s School and followed it up by acquiring Bachelor of Arts from the similar institute. He then proceeded to do the degree of Law and completed the LL.B in the year of 1935.
He then decided to take part in a political career path at an early age and became Secretary General of Muslim Students Union at his college between the time periods of 1927 till 1931. After acquiring his further education, Rahimtoola proceeded to business affairs along with being a strong supporter of the Muslim League. Rahimtoola was the founder and leader President of the Bombay Provincial Muslim Chamber of Commerce along with President Federation Muslim Chamber of Commerce Delhi.
Following the decision to partition of the sub-continent, Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola canvassed for the Muslim League in the year of 1946 elections. He was the President of the Bombay Muslim League Parliamentary Board for Local Bodies from the time period of 1945 till 1947. Simultaneously, he also sustained to be the President of the Bombay Muslim Students Union from the time period of 1946 to 1948. Jinnah had also elected him to be Senior Member of the Muslims to the Government of India Food Delegation to the UK and USA in the year of 1946.
By the month of August 1947, Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola was sent to get the Independence of Pakistan originally from the British Government. He also managed the honor of unfurling the Pakistan Flag abroad for the 1st time. Rahimtoola sustained to be the Ambassador of Pakistan to the UK during this time from 1947 till 1952. He was the leader of Pakistani delegations involving Prime Minister’s Conference London 1948, Inter-Allied Repatriation Agency 1948, International Trade and Employment Conference Geneva 1948, Freedom of the Press Conference 1948, South East Asian Conference on Colombo Plan 1950, Commonwealth Finance Ministers Conference 1949, G.A.T.T. Conference 1951 and Commonwealth Finance Minister’s Conference 1952.
In the year of 1952 Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola officially made part of the proclamation of Queen Elizabeth II to the throne of the UK. In the similar year, he was elected as Ambassador to France where he stayed till the end of the year 1953. During his era, in the year of 1953 he led the Pakistani Delegation to UNESCO in the state of Paris.
By the year of 1953, Rahimtoola came back to Pakistan and took designation as Governor of Sindh. He managed the post for merely over a year till the end of the year 1954. In the year 1954, he was moved to Punjab as Governor.

Other political offices

Apart from being the Governor of Sindh and Governor of Punjab, he was appointed as the Federal Minister of Commerce and Industries of Pakistan, chairman Pakistan Red Cross. He sustained to be in public service until his retirement in the time period of late 1970s.

Business affairs

In the time period of late 1950s, he stepped into the private business by setting up an investment company known as Bandenawaz (Pvt) Ltd. Habib also was elected as Minister in several capacities of Commerce, Industries and Petroleum. He also managed the Pakistan trade delegations to the Afro Asian Conference in the Bundung 1955 and British East Africa in the year of 1956. After the announcement of Martial Law in the year of 1958 under the regime of General Ayub Khan, Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola withdrew from the field of active politics. He was though elected as the Chairman of the newly made Karachi Development Authority in the year of 1958 – as post he held till the year of 1960.
During the time period of 1960s, Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola ventured and concentrated into industrial business. He was the Chairman Board of Directors of various reputable firms namely British Oxygen Limited, United Bank Limited, Pakistan Cables Limited, Bandenawaz Limited, Brooke Bond Limited, Aspro Nicholas Pakistan Limited, Metal Box [Pakistan] Limited, Chambon Pakistan Limited, Coates Brothers Pakistan Limited, Organon Pakistan Limited.
Following the separation of the state East Pakistan in the year of 1971, Habib Ibrahim Rahimtoola’s business interests suffered a huge setback. He although sustained his support for Pakistan serving as Chair of the Pakistan Red Cross on the insistence of then President Yahya Khan followed by the regime of Zulfiqar Ali Bhutto. Mr. Rahimtoola was granted the Pakistan Movement Gold Medal by Prime Minister Junejo in the year of 1987.
He also was appointed on the Boards of several multinational and national companies in the state of Pakistan as either a Director or Chairman including: United Bank Ltd, Brooke Bond, Eastern Federal Insurance, Pakistan Oxygen (now BOC Pakistan), Organon Pakistan (part of AkzoNobel), Aspro-Nicholas (now Reckitt Benckiser) and Pakistan Cables (BICC).

Personal life

On the family front, his father Ibrahim Rahimtoola was a famous Indian reformer and parliamentarian. His Uncle Jafar Rahimtoola and Cousin Hoosenaly Rahimtoola both were very notable Bombay politicians and sustained to be Mayors accordingly. His own brother Fazal Rahimtoola sustained to be an active politician pre and post partition of the sub-continent from the state of India. Habib married Zubeida Sultan Chinoy in the year of 1936. They had 2 sons and 1 daughter from marriage.
